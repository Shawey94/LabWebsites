Medical Imaging and Neuroscientific Discovery Laboratory (MIND)

The objective of our lab is to move forward the state of the art in multimodal brain image analysis, in terms of analysis methodologies, algorithms, validation approaches, benchmark datasets, and neuroscience.

Our research interests

Research on Healthy Brain

A major goal of this research topic is to understand the organizational architecture of human brain functions and its relationship to brain structures. Currently, brain imaging is the only noninvasive way to study our brain. This work will have an important significance for the diagnosis and treatment of many diseases.

Read more

Disease Diagnosis

A major goal of this research topic is to find clinical diagnostic basis for some brain diseases. For some brain diseases that currently lack reliable clinical diagnostic basis, we expected to use machine learning models and methods to find disease characteristics in a large number of disease brain images and healthy brain images, and provide a reliable basis for clinical diagnosis.

Read more

Image Synthesis

A major goal of this research topic is to use one kind of brain image to create another kind of Brain Image. Different kinds of Brian Images have different functions. Sometimes, some kinds of brain images are highly desired but the scanning is bad for patientsâ€™ health or the images are lost. To solve this kind of problems, we aimed to train a model that can use one kind of brain image which is easy to obtain and harmless to create another kind of images.

Read more

Machine Learning models in Brain Image

A major goal of this research topic is using the mature algorithms and models in the machine learning field, and combined with the domain knowledge of brain images, to design a new effective model for brain image research.
